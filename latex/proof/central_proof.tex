Our residual is given by

\begin{equation}
	R_f = f(Y_{t + \Delta t}) - f(X_t) - \int_t^{t+\Delta t}\mathcal{L_\theta}f(X_s)ds
\end{equation}

so we find that

\begin{equation}
	\mathbb{E}[R_f] = \mathbb{E}_{p_t + \Delta t}[f] - \mathbb{E}_{p_t}[f] - \Delta t \mathbb{E}_{p_t}[\mathcal{L}_\theta f]
\end{equation}

and so if $\mathbb{E}[R_f]$ is minimized, then 

\begin{equation}
	\frac{\mathbb{E}_{p_t + \Delta t}[f] - \mathbb{E}_{p_t}[f]}{\Delta t} = \mathbb{E}_{p_t}[\mathcal{L}_\theta f]
\end{equation}

But the Fokker-Planck equation now gives us that

\begin{equation}
	\frac{d}{dt}\mathbb{E}[f(X_t)] = \mathbb{E}[\mathcal{L}f(X_t)]
\end{equation}

so minimizing the residual enforces

\begin{equation}
	\mathbb{E}[\mathcal{L}_\theta f] = \mathbb{E}_{p_t}[\mathcal{L}f]
\end{equation}
